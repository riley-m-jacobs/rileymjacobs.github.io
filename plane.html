About

The company, Assuming Incompressible, set out to build a plane that could smash records in design. We did half of that -- but we did build the plane. The process was intensely detailed, and our customer gave us many requirements to ensure our system met performance standards. Ultimately we were able to complete the mission and scored decently compared to previous teams. Yet we know we could do better: if we had the knowledge now that we did at the start of Aircraft Design I, our plane may have looked drastically different.

Aircraft Design is not a simple thing. It challenged our critical thinking skills, problem solving skills, teamwork, communication, motivation, and knowledge. Each design choice, no matter how small, impacts every other system. But Design was incredibly rewarding, more so than any other class we have ever taken. Assuming Incompressible learned more, planned more, did more, and slept less than we ever thought possible from one class. Yet we came out of it prepared for industry: ready to take Lockheed, Boeing, and grad school by storm. We developed skills that we will use in our work and made lifelong friends. Turns out, shared trauma really does bring people together. We all wish we had started talking to each other earlier in college!
