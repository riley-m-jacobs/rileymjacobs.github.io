Work

Riley Jacobs is a current full-time Aerospace Engineering Doctoral candidate at the University of Texas at Austin. Her research and academic interests are focused specifically in aerothermodynamics and fluid mechanics. Ms Jacobs’ experimental work is focused on optical diagnostics especially laser-based measurements. Her research will be applied to the study of standing denotation shock waves and hypersonic flows.

Ms Jacobs is pursuing a career focused on developing solutions to nonequilibrium and hypersonic fluids. With the help of her graduate degree, she will push the boundaries of complex fluid flow. Her ultimate career goal is to transform commercial aerodynamic flight by improving safety and industry standards.
